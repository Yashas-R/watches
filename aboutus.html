Our Story · Est. Geneva 1977

Crafting eternity,
one timepiece
at a time.

Founded in Geneva in 1977 by master watchmaker Henri Leclerc, Horologium was born from a singular obsession: to create watches that transcend utility and become heirlooms for future generations.

Every watch in our atelier passes through the hands of at least twelve specialists before it reaches a collector. We spend an average of 340 hours on each piece — not because we must, but because we believe excellence demands nothing less.

Today, Horologium watches reside in private collections across 18 countries, worn by those who understand that true luxury is measured not in price, but in the depth of craft behind each tick.

Our Journey

Four decades of excellence

1977
Founded in Geneva
Henri Leclerc establishes Horologium SA on Rue du Rhône, Geneva, with a team of four watchmakers and a vision to challenge established maisons.
1983
First In-House Calibre
The Cal. H-001 is unveiled — a hand-wound movement with 52-hour power reserve. It wins the Grand Prix d'Horlogerie de Genève.
1992
Perpetuel Classique Launched
Our perpetual calendar complication debuts and becomes a cornerstone of the collection, recognised as one of the finest of its generation.
2001
Expansion to Tokyo & Dubai
Horologium opens its first boutiques outside Switzerland, bringing the Geneva atelier experience to collectors in Asia and the Middle East.
2012
The Tourbillon Milestone
The Héritage Tourbillon debuts with a flying tourbillon at 6 o'clock and a 78-hour power reserve, cementing Horologium among the great complication makers.
2020
Cosmos Orrery — 12 Pieces
The most complex watch Horologium has ever produced: a miniature solar system on the wrist. All 12 pieces are placed with private collectors in weeks.
2025
Online Flagship Launch
We bring the full Horologium experience to the world with our online flagship, offering every timepiece with white-glove delivery and lifetime service.
The People Behind the Craft

Our Masters

H
Henri Leclerc
Founder & Master Watchmaker
With 50 years in haute horlogerie, Henri's pursuit of perfection has shaped every principle Horologium stands for. His hands have touched every calibre we have ever released.
I
Isabelle Moreau
Head of Dial Artistry
Trained in the enamel workshops of Le Locle, Isabelle brings a painterly sensibility to our dials. Each of her grand feu enamel pieces requires three weeks of dedicated work.
R
Romain Dupuis
Chief Movement Engineer
A graduate of WOSTEP with two patents in escapement design, Romain leads a team of 12 engineers continuously pushing the boundaries of mechanical accuracy.
S
Sophie Aubert
Head of Case Design
Sophie studied industrial design in Milan before joining Horologium. Her sculptural approach to case geometry gives each watch a distinctive architectural presence.
M
Marc Tissot
Master Angleur & Finisher
Marc has spent 28 years perfecting the art of anglage — the hand-beveling of movement components. He polishes more than 40 individual parts per watch.
A
Amara Osei
Director of Client Relations
Amara ensures every Horologium collector receives a relationship that lasts a lifetime. She personally oversees white-glove delivery for our most significant commissions.
